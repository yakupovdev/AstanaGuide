Upcoming cultural and social events in Astana

Backstreet Boys

21 September 2025

For the first time ever, the legendary Backstreet Boys are coming to Astana for a long-awaited concert. This is an event you simply cannot miss!

Astana Arena is one of the best stadiums in Central Asia, with comfortable seats and the atmosphere of a true world-class show. The arena's retractable roof allows concerts to be held in any weather, maintaining the comfort and magic of the evening. Concerts of this scale fill stadiums around the world, and now, on September 21, it will happen here in Astana.


It's nostalgia, emotions, memories, and energy that you'll never forget. On stage, there will be five charismatic artists, powerful live performances, spectacular lighting, world-class sound, and thousands of spectators united by one music.

PGL Astana 2026

15 May 2026

Following the resounding success of PGL Astana in 2025, when spectators in the Kazakh capital witnessed an exciting Tier-1 Counter-Strike tournament and celebrated Team Spirit's victory, PGL is back with another must-see event: PGL Astana 2026!

From May 9 to 17, 2026, 16 elite teams will gather: 12 will receive direct invitations based on the Global VRS ranking 4 will go through open and closed qualifiers
The final stage of the tournament will take place from May 15 to 17 at Barys Arena, giving Kazakhstani fans a unique opportunity to see the world's strongest teams live at one of the most prestigious international CS2 tournaments.

Don't miss your chance to be part of history! Tickets for PGL Astana 2026 are already on sale. Let's recreate the dream atmosphere of 2025 together!

Vienna Strauss Philharmonie Orchestra

October 20 2025

For the first time in its history, the Vienna Strauss Philharmonie Orchestra is going on tour in Kazakhstan and will perform concerts in Almaty and Astana.

The program includes the best works of world classics — masterpieces by Mozart, Brahms, Haydn, Dvořák, Rossini, as well as legendary waltzes and polkas by the great Strauss dynasty, whose name is inextricably linked with Vienna.

The orchestra consists of 27 musicians from different countries around the world: Austria, Hungary, Japan, Ukraine, Germany, Poland, Taiwan, Croatia, and Cyprus. Each of them was carefully selected by an expert jury for this special tour.

The main mission of the ensemble is to preserve and develop Viennese musical culture and promote it in Europe and on the international stage. The concert in Astana will be a highlight of the fall, where the audience will be able to enjoy the energy of Viennese traditions and feel the spirit of true classical Vienna.
